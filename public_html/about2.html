Later Stages Of The Internet (1998-2004)


The Internet And Advancements

E-Commerce

Companies started advertising their products online and by 1999 global e-commerce reaches $150 billion. This way of buying things online was becoming more and more popular, and some companies emerged to take advantage of this golden opportunity such as Amazon and eBay.

Top E-commerce companies

  • Amazon
  • eBay

Amazon

  • 1994- Founded by Jeff Bezos (Figure.9)
  • He started thinking of ideal things to sell
  • First things on the list are books

eBay

  • 1995- Founded by Pierre Omidyar (Figure.10)
  • Pierre Omidyar and Jeff Bezos thought of the web as a place for business
  • Create a marketplace to be very efficient

E-Commerce Security

  • Upgraded needed for customer's information
  • Public Key Cryptography offered a more secure way
  • Encrypting the data that the sender sends to the receiver
  • Private Key is the combination used by the receiver to decrypt the data

People and Web

Broadcasting

  • People have control over what they want to broadcast
  • YouTube
  • 2015- Founded by Jawed Karim, Chad Hurley (Figure.11) , and Steve Chen
  • one of the popular broadcasting websites
  • People share videos and comment about them

News

  • Millions of people looking for great news to expose to the community
  • Digg
  • 2004- Founded by Kevin Rose (Figure.12) and Jay Adelson
  • A website that enables its users to share interesting stories and news on the internet
  • People share the news
  • Decide what is important to publish

Social networking

  • Facebook
  • 2004- Founded by Mark Zuckerberg (Figure.13) , Eduardo Saverin, Andrew McCollum, Dustin Moskovitz, and Chris Hughes, roommates and students at Harvard College
  • Maps all relationships that people have

Music sharing

  • Napster
  • 1999- founded by Shawn Fanning (Figure.18) and Sean Parker
  • Free music = Napster
  • Shared digital audio files in MP3 format
  • Fastest spreading software ever
  • 2001- a verdict was announced that Napster had to be shut down due to illegal file-sharing

Conclusion

ARPANET was mainly developed as a government defense tool and isolated to few universities and government projects, and never meant to be used for personal matters. However, the World Wide Web made the Internet more accessible to everyone in the world. Everything that started as a government investment and an expensive project is now one of the best inventions of humankind. Many companies developed, and took advantage of this computer network and became what they are today. During the last decades, the Internet has shown how effective and crucial is for our lives, and how connected society is thanks to this innovative technology
